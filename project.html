Projects

Menlo Innovations

By implementing FOOBB, Menlo Innovations’ leadership hoped to foster employee engagement. FOOBB was intended to promote transparency and encourage employees to proactively make informed business decisions based on accurate financial knowledge. However, employees faced issues with comprehension.



Process

  • Project & Client Management
  • Collaborative Work
  • User Interviews & Observations
  • Qualitative User-Centered Analysis
  • Data Synthesis
  • Consulting

Overview

Employees at Menlo Innovations had difficulties understanding and engaging with the numbers presented in the company’s Open Book Management (OBM) presented in the form of a whiteboard that tracks the company’s financial metrics called Front Office Open Book Board (FOOBB). To assess employees’ understanding of FOOBB and gaps in execution of the OBM method, we conducted ethnographic observations, interviews, and research to provide our findings and recommendations. We found that there were issues with the presentation of financial data on the board, different levels of financial understanding among employees, unclear expectations, negative impressions of FOOBB meetings, and inefficient transitions between financial quarters.
